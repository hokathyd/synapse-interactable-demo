Glutamatergic Synapse

The brain's primary excitatory communication system

What is a glutamatergic synapse?

Synapses are the primary way information is transmitted across neurons. In glutamatergic synapses, the neurotransmitter is glutamate: the main excitatory signal for ~90% of brain synapses.

The presynaptic axon terminal stores glutamate in vesicles. An action potential triggers Ca²⁺ to enter, which causes vesicles to fuse and release glutamate into the synaptic cleft.

Glutamate then binds AMPA and NMDA receptors onto the postsynaptic dendritic spine, exciting the cell and triggering long-term potentiation (LTP) which is the basis of learning and memory.
