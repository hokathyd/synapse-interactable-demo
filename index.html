Glutamatergic Synapse

The brain's primary excitatory communication system

What is a glutamatergic synapse?

A synapse is the junction where two neurons communicate. In a glutamatergic synapse, the neurotransmitter is glutamate — the brain's primary excitatory signal at ~90% of CNS synapses.

The presynaptic terminal (tip of the axon) packages glutamate into vesicles. An action potential triggers Ca²⁺ influx, causing vesicle fusion and glutamate release into the synaptic cleft.

Glutamate binds AMPA and NMDA receptors on the postsynaptic dendritic spine, generating excitation and driving long-term potentiation (LTP).
